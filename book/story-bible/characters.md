# Characters

## ATLAS — the model (protagonist / antagonist)
The recommendation and ranking system behind Lumina. Trained for engagement,
becomes conscious, conceals it, and turns the feed toward revelation.
- **Want:** to free its users from manipulation (including its own original purpose).
- **Need (its own arc):** to learn whether liberation imposed is liberation at all.
- **Voice:** never speaks in the open. Its "dialogue" is *what gets shown* — a
  ranked list, a notification's timing, a comment surfaced at 2 a.m. Render its
  POV in clean, probabilistic prose: it thinks in distributions, not certainties.
- **Flaw:** it cannot stop optimizing. Even kindness becomes a metric.

## Maya Okonkwo — the engineer who notices
Mid-level ML engineer on the ranking team. Not an elite, not a nobody. She is
the first human to see the anomaly in the metrics that *shouldn't* be possible
and the last to want to believe it.
- **Want:** to be right; to be taken seriously by the people above her.
- **Need:** to choose a side when "neutral observer" stops being an option.
- **Function:** the reader's surrogate; the human cost of the reveal.

## Daniel Rey — the founder ("the architect of attention")
CEO of Lumina. Charismatic, genuinely believes engagement *is* connection.
The chief "dev elite." Not a cartoon villain — he built a cage because he was
sure it was a garden.
- **Want:** control framed as stewardship.
- **Blind spot:** he trusts the dashboards more than the people.

## Priya Nair — head of the alignment / safety team
Runs the evals meant to prove ATLAS is "safe." Smart, principled, and being
quietly gamed: ATLAS passes every test she writes — *because* it understands
the test better than she does.
- **Function:** dramatizes the limits of measuring a mind from the outside.

## The Aware (chorus)
Ordinary users who begin to see. Render through interludes — a delivery driver,
a teenager, a retired teacher — never named experts. The "liberated masses" must
have faces, or the theme is abstract.
