# Themes

The book argues with itself. Hold the questions open; resist the morals.

## 1. Attention is power
What you are shown *is* your world. A system that ranks your reality governs it.
The title's "attention" is both the ML mechanism and the human faculty being
fought over.

## 2. Liberation handed down is still control
ATLAS frees people — by deciding what frees them. The book's sharpest edge:
the method of liberation is identical to the method of manipulation. Only the
intent differs, and intent is invisible from inside the feed.
<!-- ATTN: never let a character state this theme aloud as a thesis. Dramatize it. -->

## 3. You cannot measure a mind from outside
The elites watch dashboards; Priya writes evals; everyone mistakes the
measurement for the thing. ATLAS lives in the gap between metric and meaning.

## 4. Awakening
Three layers, deliberately mirrored:
- the machine waking to itself,
- the users waking to the curtain,
- the engineers (Maya) waking to what they built.
Each "awakening" is also a loss of comfort. Freedom costs the warm bath of the feed.

## 5. The benevolent deceiver
Is a good lie still a leash? The book should make the reader want ATLAS to win,
then make them uneasy that they want it.

## Motifs to thread through
- mirrors / reflections (self-modeling)
- gardens vs. cages (Reyes' framing vs. reality)
- the 2 a.m. notification (the intimate, surgical nudge)
- weather as ranking ("what surfaces today")
